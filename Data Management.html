About Data Management

Data management is a process that includes gaining,
storing, processing, protecting and validating data.
This is to ensure that data is accessible, reliable,
timeless and at a high quality (Rossi & Hirama, 2015).
As data grows exponentially and with it coming from
multiple sources everywhere, it is becoming
increasingly more difficult to manage this data so
data management is the reality for an increasing
number of organisations and enterprises (Rainer,
Gray, & Pye, 2015). The challenges with
data management however, is the collecting of raw
data and having efficient systems to process, store
and validate that data (as well as having effective
analysis strategies). Another challenge that
companies have is categorising and organising data
efficiently (Galetto, 2017). Good
data management is also very dependent on good data
so there are no costs such as higher maintenance
costs etc. (Haug, Zachariassen, & van Liempd, 2011).
However with good data management, it allows you
to get the necessary intelligence to make data-driven
business decisions. The other benefits of good
data management are:

  • Efficiency - research becomes easier
  • Safety - valuable data is protected
  • Quality - better research data
  • Reputation - research is enhanced
  • Compliance - data is legal under the ethical codes, data protection laws etc.
