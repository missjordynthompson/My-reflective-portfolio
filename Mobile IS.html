Mobile IS


About Mobile IS

Our mobile phones and the ability to communicate
with anyone from anywhere at anytime, has changed
the way we do everything to play music, interact with
each other, navigate around etc. It is has also
significantly changed the way we go about our
everyday life and do business:

  • Manage our operations
  • Interact with customers and suppliers
  • Compete in the marketplace

    • (Love, 2013). The benefits for a business to go mobile
    are significant, therefore the consumer world are
    rapidly adapting and changing to mobile as they are
    easily movable in space, operable no matter what the
    location, and has a wireless connection (Pernici
    & Krogstie, 2008). Mobile computing was designed for
    workers who travel outside the boundaries of their
    organisations as well as anyone travelling outside
    their home (Rainer, Gray, & Pye, 2014). The
    advantages of everyone changing to mobile is that the
    demand for access to Wi-Fi is incredibly high so the
    government/council in Wellington are making it their
    mission to deliver free secure Wi-Fi using cbdfree
    (CityLink (n.d)). However the disadvantages is the
    environmental and resource constraints, such as
    certain parts of New Zealand there are no service
    therefore your mobile would be useless (Wu, Chang,
    Ho, & Chao, 2008). There are also security issues with
    hackers, lack of information control, and privacy
    issues with Mobile IS.
