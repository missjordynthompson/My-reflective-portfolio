About Customer Relationship Management

Customer relationship management (CRM) is
personal marketing for businesses in order to market
to each individual customer by using the information
of previous purchases, needs and wants etc (Rouse,
2014). CRM focuses on and is driven by the customer
improving the business relationship with the
customer but also providing a responsive, quality
service. (Rainer, Gray, & Pye, 2015). Organisations
compile information on the customer across various
channels including the companys social media,
website, email, loyalty programmes etc using a four
stage process:

  • Socialization - Communicating knowledge
    social interactions
  • Externalization - Creating knowledge that
    is shared within an organisation
  • Combination - integrating knowledge from
    different sources
  • Internalisation - taking knowledge and
    putting it into business practices

    • (Khodakarami & Chan, 2014). CRM benefits the
    customer significantly with more information
    available to them, information is catered and
    personalised, and a community is built of alike loyal
    customers. Therefore the customer is willing to allow
    data to be used inCRM which enables organisations
    to create and deliver great customer service, maintain
    a community of loyal customers, and thus creating a
    sustainable advantage over other competitors (Wang
    & Feng, 2012). However using social media as a way of
    building your relationship with customers is always a
    risk as you have to be very wary of what you put out
    there.





    My Personal Reflection

    My experience of customer relationship management
    is when I am online shopping and the items I was
    looking at the last time I visited, or items similar,
    come up. As a customer, this makes very interested
    and curious about those items. This impacts the
    buyer in a way where they stay longer on the website
    and look at some more things that they would like,
    therefore the company is making more money with
    the items they are purchasing and the publicity they
    are getting from the customer. It also happens when I
    am on social media or on my email, the clothes that I
    like and recommended items come up therefore
    making me interested and dragging me to the website
    once again. In my opinion, this technique works very
    well and keeps me coming back to the website
    because of the various platforms it has spanned out
    on.
